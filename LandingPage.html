Neo Sport - Landing Page Design (February 2023)


Overview

  • Project type: Personal
    My role: UX/UI
    Deliverables: Wireframes, Live Prototype, Landing Page Design
    Tools: Figma, Photoshop

    Live Prototype >>> https://neosport.animaapp.io/

Story

Neo Sport is an ecommerce company that offers high-quality sportswear for athletes. The company was looking to revamp its landing page in order to increase its online presence. The final product is a new landing page design that would improve user engagement and increase sales.


Challenge

The client approached me with a brief. The main goal was to drive sales and improve visual aspects of the brand. They were experiencing a high bounce rate on their landing page, and visitors were not converting into customers.


Solution

I followed a user-centred design approach to create an effective solution. Based on given research that had already been conducted by the client company, I identified the following user needs:

• Users want to see the latest gym wear products and styles (focus on fashion sense and finding the needed products right away).
• Users want to know the features and benefits of the products (pockets in leggings, focus on high quality fabrics in general).
• Users want to feel confident about their purchase decision (sense of achievement, commitment).


Approach

Based on my insights, I brainstormed ideas and came up with the following features:

• A video for a hero section that showcases the latest Neo Sport sportswear product and a call-to-action button.
• A product grid that displays the sportswear with their unique features and outlook.
• A call-to-action button on every section that directs users to view collections and make a purchase.
• A section promoting a sustainable product line to drive a positive sense of purchase.
• A social media section (Instagram) to increase online presence.

Prototyping

I created a high-fidelity mockup to test these ideas. Later on I iterated based on user feedback until I had a design that met both user needs and client goals.



Testing

I conducted user tests (such as A/B tests) to evaluate the effectiveness of the design. With the help of 5 people who volunteered to assist me in gathering more data, I was able to notice a common denominator being the sense and awareness of shopping in a high quality ecommerce shop. The “vibe” of being a customer who takes sports seriously and looks “cool”, as well as the user story of an athletic persona is the most important factor to drive the notion of adding products to a “bag” before checking out.

Outcome

The final design of the Neo Sport landing page included the following features:

• A hero section with video that showcased the latest sportswear product and style, with a clear call-to-action button that directed users to browse the collection.
• A product grid that displayed the products with their features and benefits, with clear pricing and "add to favourites" icon.
• A section promoting a sustainable product line to drive a positive sense of purchase.
• A section for Top Picks products to convey a sense of community.
• A footer with links to the company's social media pages, contact information, and a newsletter sign-up form.

The client was pleased with the final design of the landing page and launched it successfully. Over the next 2 months there was an increase in sales and a decrease in bounce rate, indicating that the new landing page design was effective in meeting user needs and goals.

Reflections

In hindsight, while working on this project, I found myself wishing for greater access to additional Figma resources such as plugins (like the Anima App). Expanding my toolkit in this way would have allowed me to further explore animation possibilities for the prototype and enhance the responsiveness of the landing page. Although I am content with the design choices I made, I yearned for the chance to delve into mobile-centered design, integrating elements from established design systems, and refining the branding elements, particularly by focusing on colour. In my opinion, these aspects would have added an extra layer of depth and cohesiveness to the overall UI design.
