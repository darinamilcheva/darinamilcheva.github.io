Group Chats feature in Tumblr App - Is it being utilised?
(User Research) (June 2021)


Update [22 September 2021] : Group Chat feature has been removed from the Tumblr App.
This official decision was announced roughly three months after my study was already done. Source: Group Chat Removal: Sept. 22, 2021

Overview

  • Project type: Personal
    My role: UX Research
    Deliverables: User Research and Analysis
    Disclaimer: I do not work for, nor am I affiliated with Tumblr, Inc.

Story

I consider myself a frequent user of the Tumblr app. Overall my experience has been good throughout the years. Nonetheless, I recently developed curiosity on how other people use it when the new “Group Chats” feature was added. Other than а difference in personal interests, was the use of it, specifically, a single shared experience? How often do others use additional features (such as “Camera”) provided in the app?


Challenge

Discover pain points of the current “Group Chats” or “Camera” features and propose solutions to improve user experience.

More information on Group Chats: Group Chats – Help Center

Camera feature: Content Editing Tools – Help Center

Approach

What makes this project unique is the ability to work with two different perspectives on the matter. While it may seem disadvantageous to possess personal user experience, causing a possible mix between user and designer, in different ways it certainly adds to an already empathetic approach to solving the problem, as well as identifying it earlier on. I operate with the intention to bridge the gap between both the users’ and the business entity’s needs.

User Survey

To start off, these features on the app are only present on the mobile version of Tumblr. Therefore part of the requirements for user testing included people who already own and use the app (not limited to whether they have iOs or Android). The data outcome of the survey is that 7 total responses were given.
Age range of participants: 18 - 30 years old
No information regarding gender.
Link to all information gathered: User Research Raw Results






Analysis and insights

None of the participants had selected the “Yes, often/sometimes.” or “I like it, it's a good feature.” option when asked about using Group Chats. This suggests lack of engagement. No one selected “I completely dislike it.” but most notably no participant chose the answer “I would prefer for Group Chats to stay the same.” This highlights not only indifference in users but the majority stating preference for an entirely different feature as opposed to optimisation of the current one. From the available data it appears users are more content with the Camera feature than the Chat Groups one. Following the principle of Usefulness = Usability + Utility, it can be assessed that the Utility element is lacking.

Constraints

The nature of this project being personal leaves me with a lack of insight on Tumblr stakeholders’ objectives. I am simply considering overall engagement in the mobile app as a necessity. Through common design practices and based on the acquired data, I can make suggestions to improve user experience.

Outcome

If I were to go into the ideation stage of problem solving for this design, while there are inclinations to the removal of Chat Groups as a whole, I would work on alternating or possibly investing in a new feature instead. For example, as offered above, swiping in the app to something like “Recommended posts” would more likely improve engagement. However, as an already launched feature, Chat Groups could also be kept (with the same name) but accessed differently and maybe connected with other already established platforms (e.g Discord, as it is popular for its community and group chatting). Having the ability to swipe left or right gives an opportunity for improved usability and should be utilised, not entirely removed. Through the available information and analysis, user pain points have been identified to be on an interaction level. In other words, while there is a user experience problem, it is not on the scale of a relationship level pain point where the brand and mission of Tumblr are at risk.

Reflections

As someone with appreciation for user centred design, my proclivity and desire to improve experience led me to empathising and ideating for this project. Nevertheless, here are some of the processes I would have done differently:

Further surveying
Collecting and showcasing more data, asking simpler but additional questions I believe would have elevated the research process and outcome.
Prototyping + Testing
I already had an idea to create a new Recommendations feature when swiping left or right but the most important step to designing is still implementing. My inability to further work on this project due to time limitations and lack of sources is taken into consideration as I can never know if this is a good design decision without proper testing, verifying and prototyping.
