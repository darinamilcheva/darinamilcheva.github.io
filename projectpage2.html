User Interface Design of Fleamarket App
(April 2022)


Overview

  • Project type: Part of a university coursework
    My role: UI & Visual Design (Logo, Concept Realisation)
    Deliverables: New Interface, Log In Page, Live Prototype

Story

The world's natural resources are finite and reducing the demand for them is becoming an important part to fight against the overuse of these raw materials. It is not just at an industrial level that this fight occurs. Reducing the general demand for products is another way to help. Many that still can be of some use end up filling the landfills. Recycling these unwanted items is becoming necessary.


Facts

• Traditionally, charity shops in the UK were able to reuse and recycle more than 90% of donated books and 85% of electrical goods.
• Councils around the UK have to pay around £100 per tonnes for waste going to these landfills.
• Reducing the use of landfill reduces the CO2 footprint.
• In 2018/2019, 339'000 tonnes of textiles where kept out of landfill just by charity retail.
• Charity shops provide items such as clothes at an affordable price.


Challenge

Design an interface for an online mobile app called FleaMarket that will enable adults to sell and buy second-hand unwanted items such as household items, equipment, tools and belongings etc.


Approach

Starting off by focusing on the "Welcome Page" and "Log in/Sign up Pages", first impressions are key. By easing in the access to the app, the goal is to make the user feel familiar and comfortable, ready to purchase or sell an item and help the environment. After I was given access to data from user research and a pre-made interface to improve upon, my task was clear.

Simplify, streamline and add visual elements that stay consistent with branding.

Despite not being a stated requirement, I believe a logo and consistent elements of visual design related to branding is integral. Consistency is key to make an interface intuitive.

User Flow

What makes this project unique is the ability to work with two different perspectives on the matter. While it may seem disadvantageous to possess personal user experience, causing a possible mix between user and designer, in different ways it certainly adds to an already empathetic approach to solving the problem, as well as identifying it earlier on. I operate with the intention to bridge the gap between both the users’ and the business entity’s needs.

Reflections

As someone with appreciation for user centred design, my proclivity and desire to improve experience led me to empathising and ideating for this project. Nevertheless, here are some of the processes I would have done differently:

Further surveying
Collecting and showcasing more data, asking simpler but additional questions I believe would have elevated the research process and outcome.
Prototyping + Testing
I already had an idea to create a new Recommendations feature when swiping left or right but the most important step to designing is still implementing. My inability to further work on this project due to time limitations and lack of sources is taken into consideration as I can never know if this is a good design decision without proper testing, verifying and prototyping.
